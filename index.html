About Me

How I Fell Into Data (And Never Looked Back)

Hey, I'm Jason. Honestly, I never thought I'd be doing this for a living. It all started when I was reading about crime stats in Victoria and couldn't stop thinking: "Why can't we see these patterns coming?" Next thing I knew, I was knee-deep in spreadsheets at 2am trying to figure it out.

Turns out, I'm pretty good at spotting things other people miss. Whether it's crime trends or why a company's losing money in ways they can't see, I get genuinely excited about digging into messy data until it makes sense. My friends think I'm weird for loving this stuff, but hey - someone's got to do it.

The best part of my job? That moment when someone looks at my analysis and goes "Oh my god, THAT'S what's been happening!" I've saved companies serious money just by pointing out patterns they walked past every day. It's like being a detective, except instead of solving crimes, I'm solving business problems.

I've been doing this for over 2 years now, got my certifications from Google, IBM, and Microsoft, but honestly? The real skill is just being curious enough to keep asking "why?" until you find something useful. That's what I bring to your data - fresh eyes and way too much enthusiasm for finding answers.

My Services

Comprehensive data analysis services tailored to your specific business needs

Data Cleaning (The Boring But Important Stuff)

Look, nobody likes cleaning data, but it's like trying to solve a puzzle with half the pieces missing if you skip this step. I make sure your data actually makes sense before we start looking for insights. Trust me, you don't want to build strategies on wonky numbers.

Finding Patterns That Actually Matter

This is where it gets fun. I dig through your data looking for the "why" behind what's happening. Sometimes it's obvious, sometimes it's weird and unexpected. Either way, I'll find the patterns that can help you make better decisions.

Making Data Make Sense

Numbers in spreadsheets are hard to get excited about. Charts and graphs that tell a clear story? That's how you get people to actually pay attention to what your data is saying. I turn confusing analysis into "oh, now I get it" moments.

Dashboards That Don't Suck

Most dashboards are either too complicated or don't show anything useful. I build ones that actually help you spot problems early and catch opportunities before you miss them. Think less "wall of charts," more "here's what you need to know right now."
